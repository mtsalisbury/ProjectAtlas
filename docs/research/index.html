Research program

What Atlas is testing

Atlas does not begin by assuming that a new product or protocol is required. It first asks whether existing capabilities can be composed around a person-controlled coordination boundary.

01

Architecture boundary

Determine whether Atlas is a layer, control plane, reference architecture or unnecessary abstraction.

02

Identity and trust

Test how credentials, relationships, assertions and revocation can remain portable without becoming universal identity.

03

Context separation

Explore how one person can maintain distinct personal, employment, financial, public and pseudonymous contexts.

04

Connectivity

Assess how secure transport, mesh networking and provider mobility relate to a persistent personal presence.

05

Privacy and abuse

Identify correlation, tracking, coercion, recovery and governance risks before proposing implementation.

06

Prior art

Compare Atlas with IAM, PKI, wallets, VPN, ZTNA, decentralized identity and standards work that may already solve parts of the problem.

Open technical review

The objective is to discover where Atlas is wrong.

Project Atlas is seeking technical criticism before implementation. Useful review includes existing systems that already solve the problem, unsafe assumptions, architectural failure modes, privacy risks and experiments that could invalidate the thesis.

Questions for reviewers

  • Which existing architecture already provides this role?
  • Where does Atlas duplicate IAM, VPN, ZTNA, mesh or wallet systems?
  • Which privacy or governance assumption is unsafe?
  • What is the smallest test that could validate or reject the proposal?
