The question

Is the Internet missing a foundational layer?

Authentication, VPNs, Zero Trust, identity providers, certificates, device posture, and access control have improved. Yet a person’s digital presence remains fragmented across accounts, applications, networks, and institutions.

Atlas does not begin by proposing another replacement product. It asks whether existing technologies can be organized around a persistent, portable, person-centred Internet presence.

Research principles

Use what exists. Challenge what is missing.

01

Person-centred

The individual is the persistent point of reference—not the provider.

02

Interoperable

Atlas should leverage existing identity, network, security, and cloud systems.

03

Privacy-preserving

Recognition must not require universal disclosure, tracking, or centralized control.

04

Open to failure

The research must expose assumptions and accept that parts of the thesis may be wrong.

Open technical review

The objective is not to prove Atlas correct.

The objective is to discover where it is wrong.

Useful criticism includes

  • What already exists?
  • Which assumptions are incorrect?
  • Where would this architecture fail?
  • What privacy, governance, or abuse risks are missing?
  • Which standards should Atlas build upon?
